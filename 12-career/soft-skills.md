# Soft Skills for Software Engineers

## Why Soft Skills Matter
- 50%+ of job is communication
- Code is read more than written
- Teams ship products, not individuals
- Career growth requires influence

## Technical Communication

### Writing Design Documents
**Structure**:
1. **Context**: Why this matters
2. **Goals**: What we're trying to achieve
3. **Non-Goals**: What we're NOT doing
4. **Proposed Solution**: The approach
5. **Alternatives Considered**: What else was evaluated
6. **Open Questions**: What needs discussion

**Tips**:
- Write for your audience (eng vs PM vs exec)
- Use diagrams (architecture, sequence, flow)
- Be concise but complete
- Get feedback early

### Code Reviews

**As Reviewer**:
- Be kind and constructive
- Explain the "why" behind feedback
- Approve trivial changes quickly
- Distinguish: suggestions vs blockers

**As Author**:
- Keep PRs small (<400 lines)
- Provide context in description
- Respond to feedback gracefully
- Don't take comments personally

**Example Good Comment**:
> "Consider using a Map here instead of filter+find. It's O(n) instead of O(nÂ²) and more readable."

**Example Bad Comment**:
> "This is wrong. Use a Map."

### Technical Presentations
- Start with the "why"
- Use visuals (demos > slides)
- Practice beforehand
- Handle questions with grace
- Admit when you don't know

## Teamwork

### Collaboration
- Over-communicate (especially remote)
- Share knowledge proactively
- Help teammates without being asked
- Credit others publicly

### Conflict Resolution
- Address issues early
- Focus on problem, not person
- Seek to understand first
- Find win-win solutions

### Giving Feedback
- Be specific and actionable
- Praise in public, critique in private
- Use "I" statements ("I noticed..." vs "You always...")
- Focus on behavior, not personality

### Receiving Feedback
- Listen without defending
- Ask clarifying questions
- Thank the person
- Reflect and act on it

## Remote Work Effectiveness

### Communication
- Over-communicate status
- Use video when possible
- Respect time zones
- Document decisions

### Productivity
- Set boundaries (work hours)
- Dedicated workspace
- Take breaks
- Block focus time

### Building Relationships
- Virtual coffee chats
- Non-work channels (hobbies, pets)
- Attend virtual events
- In-person meetups when possible

## Mentoring

### As a Mentor
- Share your experiences
- Provide actionable advice
- Connect mentees with opportunities
- Regular check-ins

### Finding a Mentor
- Look internally first
- Be specific about what you need
- Respect their time
- Show appreciation and follow-through

## Time Management

### Prioritization
- Urgent vs important matrix
- Say no to low-value work
- Delegate when appropriate
- Focus on impact, not activity

### Avoiding Burnout
- Work sustainable hours
- Take PTO regularly
- Exercise and sleep
- Hobbies outside tech

## Leadership (for seniors)

### Technical Leadership
- Set technical direction
- Mentor junior engineers
- Drive architectural decisions
- Advocate for technical debt paydown

### Influence Without Authority
- Build trust and credibility
- Understand stakeholder needs
- Articulate business impact
- Form alliances

---

**Remember**: Technical skills get you hired. Soft skills get you promoted.
